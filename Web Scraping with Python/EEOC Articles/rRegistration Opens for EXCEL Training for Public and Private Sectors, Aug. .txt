<h1>Registration Opens for EXCEL Training for Public and Private Sectors, Aug. 10-13 in Washington</h1>
</div>
<div align="left"><p style="text-align: center"><em><strong>EEOC's 18th Annual Conference to Celebrate 50th Anniversary of Commission</strong></em></p>
<p>WASHINGTON -- The U.S. Equal Employment Opportunity Commission (EEOC) today announced that registration recently opened for its 18th annual EXCEL training conference on preventing employment discrimination in both the public and private
sectors.  The event takes place Aug. 10-13 at the Washington Hilton in the nation's capital.</p>
<p>EXCEL, which stands for Examining Conflicts in Employment Laws, is the EEOC's premier training conference for professionals.  It includes separate agendas for the federal sector and private sector, which includes state and local
governments.  The comprehensive training workshops and events are geared toward EEO managers, supervisors, practitioners, HR professionals, attorneys, ADR specialists and other interested parties.  </p>
<p>The theme of this year's EXCEL, which coincides with the landmark 50th anniversary of the Commission this summer, is: </p>
<p><strong>The EEOC @ 50: Building on a Legacy, Looking to Future Innovation</strong></p>
<p>Further details about the conference, including the agendas, pricing information and online registration are available at <a href="http://www.eeotraining.eeoc.gov/EXCEL2015/index.htm" id="http://www.eeotraining.eeoc.gov/EXCEL2015/index.htm|">http://www.eeotraining.eeoc.gov/EXCEL2015/index.htm</a>.</p>
<p>Registration information may also be obtained by calling 216-522-4794 or via e-mail: <a href="mailto:eeoc.traininginstitute@eeoc.gov" id="mailto:eeoc.traininginstitute@eeoc.gov|">eeoc.traininginstitute@eeoc.gov</a>.</p>
<p>The EEOC is responsible for enforcing federal laws prohibiting employment discrimination in the public and private sectors.  Additional information about the agency can be found at www.eeoc.gov.  Follow the EXCEL Conference on Twitter
at @EEOC_EXCEL.<br/>
