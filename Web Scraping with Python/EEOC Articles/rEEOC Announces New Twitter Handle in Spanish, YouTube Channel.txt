<h1>EEOC Announces New Twitter Handle in Spanish, YouTube Channel</h1>
</div>
<div align="left"><p>WASHINGTON -- The U.S. Equal Employment Opportunity Commission (EEOC) has launched a Twitter account for Spanish speakers to follow news and information about the EEOC, the agency announced today.</p>
<p>Members of the public and media can now follow <a href="https://twitter.com/EEOCespanol" id="https://twitter.com/EEOCespanol|">@EEOCespañol</a> on Twitter for information on the laws enforced by the EEOC, the charge handling process, outreach and
educational programs in Spanish.</p>
<p>"Our Twitter en Español handle provides us with another tool to interact with the Spanish-speaking public and share with them helpful information about workplace discrim­ination," said Kimberly Smith-Brown, the EEOC's director of
communications.<span> </span> "This is another example of the agency's commitment to remain accessible to the public and provide good customer service."</p>
<p>The public can also follow <a href="https://twitter.com/EEOCNews" id="https://twitter.com/EEOCNews|">@EEOCNews</a> for EEOC news in English.</p>
<p>The EEOC has also unveiled its <a href="http://www.youtube.com/user/TheEEOC/" id="http://www.youtube.com/user/TheEEOC/|">YouTube channel</a>, adding several informational and historical videos in both English and Spanish, aimed at educating
viewers about the laws the agency enforces.</p>
<p>The EEOC is the federal government agency responsible for enforcing anti-discrimination laws in the workplace. Further information about the EEOC is available at <a href="http://www.eeoc.gov/">www.eeoc.gov</a>.</p></div>
