<h1>EEOC Chair Yang, Labor Secretary Perez, and Transportation Secretary Foxx to Speak at  Annual Excel Conference</h1>
</div>
<div align="left"><p style="text-align: center;"><strong><em>August 10-13, Washington, D.C., Event Highlights EEOC's 50th Anniversary</em></strong></p>
<p>WASHINGTON - U.S. Secretary of Labor Thomas Perez and U.S. Secretary of Transportation Anthony Foxx will join Jenny R. Yang, Chair of the U.S. Equal Employment Opportunity Commission (EEOC) in addressing participants at EEOC's 18th annual EXCEL
conference, August 10 - 13 in Washington, D.C.</p>
<p>EXCEL, which stands for <em>Examining Conflicts in Employment Laws</em>, is EEOC's premier training event for professionals working to prevent employment discrimination in the public and private sectors. Its nationally recognized speakers,
hands-on workshops, comprehensive education sessions, and much-anticipated networking opportunities are designed specifically for EEO managers, supervisors, and practitioners; HR professionals; attorneys; and alternative dispute resolution
specialists. In commemoration of the agency's 50th anniversary, the theme of this year's EXCEL conference is: <strong><em>The EEOC @ 50: Building on a Legacy, Looking to Future Innovation</em></strong>.</p>
<p>In addition to Chair Yang, Secretary Foxx, and Secretary Perez, this year's EXCEL participants will hear from:</p>
<ul>
<li>EEOC Commissioners Chai Feldblum and Victoria Lipnic</li>
<li><a href="http://www.apbspeakers.com/speaker/charlayne-hunter-gault">Charlayne Hunter-Gault</a>, award-winning journalist, author, and the first African-American woman to attend the University of Georgia</li>
<li><a href="http://www.washingtonspeakers.com/speakers/biography.cfm?SpeakerID=7898">Aneesh Chopra</a>, former U.S. chief technology officer, author, and co-founder of Hunch Analytics</li>
</ul>
<p>"The 2015 EXCEL conference provides all who work on issues of equal employment opportunity a forum <em>to reflect</em> on the significant progress made in the 50 years since EEOC opened its doors and <em>to prepare</em> for the work ahead
addressing new and continuing forms of discrimination," said Chair Yang.</p>
<p>Further details about the conference, including the agenda, pricing information, and online registration, is available at <a href="http://www.eeotraining.eeoc.gov/excelmain.html" id="http://www.eeotraining.eeoc.gov/excelmain.html|">http://www.eeotraining.eeoc.gov/excelmain.html</a>.</p>
<p>You also can obtain registration information by calling (216) 522-4794 or e-mailing: <a href="mailto:eeoc.traininginstitute@eeoc.gov">eeoc.traininginstitute@eeoc.gov</a>.</p>
<p>EEOC is responsible for enforcing federal laws prohibiting employment discrimination in the public and private sectors. Additional information about the agency can be found at <a href="http://www.eeoc.gov">www.eeoc.gov</a>. </p>
