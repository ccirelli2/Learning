<h1>New ‘Digest Of EEO Law’ Issued By EEOC</h1>
</div>
<div align="left"><p style="text-align: center;"><strong><em>Includes Key Federal Sector Decisions, Special Article on Determining Status of Complainants in the EEO Process</em></strong></p>
<p>WASHINGTON -- The U.S. Equal Employment Opportunity Commission (EEOC) today announced the latest edition of its federal sector <em>Digest of Equal Employment Opportunity Law</em>, which is available online <a href="http://www.eeoc.gov/federal/digest/index.cfm">http://www.eeoc.gov/federal/digest/index.cfm</a>.</p>
<p>This edition (Fiscal Year 2016, Volume 2) features a special article entitled, <a href="http://www.eeoc.gov/federal/digest/vol_2_fy2016.cfm"><em>Stating a Claim in the EEO Process: Determining One's Status as Either an Employee or Independent
Contractor</em></a>.</p>
<p>"The article on determining the status of a complainant, as either a federal employee or contractor, is a 'must read' for the federal sector EEO community," said Carlton M. Hadden, director of EEOC's Office of Federal Operations (OFO). "This
article is a great resource and identifies recent case law on this issue."</p>
<p>The <em>EEO Digest</em>, a quarterly publication prepared by OFO, features a wide variety of recent Commission decisions and federal court cases of interest. The <em>Digest</em> also includes hyperlinks so that stakeholders can easily access the
full decisions which have been summarized. </p>
<p>This edition of the <em>Digest</em> contains summaries of noteworthy decisions issued by the EEOC, including cases involving: Agency Processing, Findings of the Merits, Attorney's Fees, Class Complaints, Remedies, Compensatory Damages, Sanctions,
Dismissals, Settlement Agreements, Stating a Claim, Summary Judgment and Timeliness.</p>
<p>The summaries are neither intended to be exhaustive or definitive as to the selected subject matter, nor are they to be given the legal weight of case law in citations. In addition to the quarterly Digest, Commission federal sector decisions are
available on EEOC's website <a href="http://eeoc.gov/federal/decisions.cfm">http://eeoc.gov/federal/decisions.cfm</a></p>
<p>The public may also receive federal sector information updates and news items via Twitter: <strong>@EEOC_OFO</strong>.</p>
<p>The EEOC enforces federal laws prohibiting employment discrimination in the public and private sectors. Further information about EEOC is available online at <a href="http://www.eeoc.gov">www.eeoc.gov</a>.</p></div>
